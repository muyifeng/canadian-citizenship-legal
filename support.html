Canada Citizenship 2026 is an independent study app for the Canadian citizenship test, with practice questions, timed mock exams, and bilingual lessons. It works fully offline, has no accounts or sign-up, and shows no ads. Below are answers to the most common questions.

Frequently asked questions

How do I restore a purchase on a new device?

Open the App, go to Profile, and tap Restore purchases. Sign in with the same Apple ID (iOS) or Google account (Android) you used to buy. Weekly, Monthly, and Lifetime unlocks all restore this way on the same store account. Purchases do not transfer between the App Store and Google Play, because each store keeps its own receipt.

How do I manage or cancel my subscription?

Subscriptions are billed by Apple or Google, not by us. In the App, open Profile → Manage subscription, which opens your store’s subscription settings. You can also go directly to Settings → your name → Subscriptions on iOS, or the Subscriptions section of the Google Play app on Android. The one-time Lifetime unlock is not a subscription and does not renew.

What’s free, and what does Pro include?

Practice questions and lessons are free. Pro unlocks unlimited mock tests and unlimited practice, available as a Weekly or Monthly subscription or a one-time Lifetime unlock. Prices are shown in the App and vary by region.

Does the App work offline?

Yes. All content is stored on your device, so you can study without a connection. Your progress, test date, and settings stay on your device.

Can I study in French?

Yes. The App is bilingual — switch between English and French at any time in Profile → Language.

How do I export or delete my data?

Your study data lives only on your device. In Profile you can export your data to a file at any time, or clear local data to delete it. Uninstalling the App also removes it. See our Privacy Policy for details.

Is the App affiliated with the Government of Canada?

No. Canada Citizenship 2026 is an independent study aid and is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or connected to the Government of Canada or Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC). “Discover Canada” / “Découvrir le Canada” is a publication of IRCC, referenced for study purposes only.
